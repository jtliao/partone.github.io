Some clarifications

  • This is designed for use on a PC
  • You can use the arrow keys, mouse wheel, left button drag, and right button drag to explore the solar system
  • The sizes of the planets are proportional to each other
  • The orbital speeds and rotational speeds of the planets are proportional to each other
  • The orbital speeds and rotational speeds are not on the same scale (1 earth orbit != 365 earth rotations)
  • The sun's size is proportional to nothing, it's just bigger than everything else
  • Distances from the sun are abritrary
  • Uranus and Venus rotate on their axis clockwise, unlike the other planets
  • For the sake of visibility, Mars' moons are larger than they should be
  • The moon's orbit speeds are proportional to each other but to nothing else
  • Jupiter, your-anus Uranus, Neptune, and Pluto have a few of moons each, only big moons (d > 1000km) are included here though
  • If your screen is really dusty, it looks like there are stars too
  • The speed of rotation of the moons on their own axis is arbitrary, the orbital rotation speed is proportional though
  • Pluto is out there somewhere
